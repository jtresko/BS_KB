# What Is a Honey Badger Day (HBD)?

A Honey Badger Day is a unique signal you get from BigShort that you can't get anywhere else. It means that there is a lot of shorting in the market, which means that all of those shorts need to buy to cover, which creates bullish pressure in the market. That bullish pressure tends to present in the latter half of the day. 

üìñ **Getting Technical:** A successful honey badger day means that SPY will reach either today‚Äôs open price or yesterday‚Äôs close price, whichever is lower, within the last 2 hours of trading.

# How to Tell if It's a Honey Badger Day

The honey badger signal comes out daily between 6-7pm PST at which point it can be seen by all Elite Tier customers on the Squeeze Meter tab in BigShort. Honey Badger Days are scored, with the meter showing how strong of a Badger the day be. Note that although it is mathematically possible, there has never been a Godly Badger Day (lvl 5). 

Overall, Honey Badger Days have a 90% historical success rate, so even though a weak HBD might sound tame, it can still be incredibly powerful. 

üìù **Note:** The Honey Badger indicator only applies to SPY.

**Late morning/Afternoon:** Recovery begins.

**Late Day:** Rally, with price going above either today‚Äôs open price or yesterday‚Äôs close price (whichever is lower) within the last 2 hours of trading.

# What about "Badgerish" Days?

There are days that are ALMOST official Honey Badger Days that we call "Badgerish Days". These days are noted in the Squeeze Meter. They trade the same way, but are less of a sure thing. Expect buying and bullish opportunities, but be more cautious and quicker to cover if the position moves against you.

# Key Takeaways

  * HBDs are SPY-specific. Only SPY and SPY derivatives should be used for this trade

  * Expect one of the three price patterns (recognizing which "shape" the day is likely to have early on can help you trade it)

  * Late-day rallies often occur due to short covering and provide great exit liquidity.

  * Use [Net Option Flow](https://Net), [Dark Pools](https://help.bigshort.com/en/articles/10280483-understanding-dark-pools-and-darkflow-in-bigshort), [Momo and SmartFlow](https://help.bigshort.com/en/articles/10361455-understanding-smartflow-and-momoflow-indicators) indicators to guide exact entries and exits.

  * Avoid shorting on HBD - very dangerous.
